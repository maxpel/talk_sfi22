Sentiment Analysis

Has gotten a somewhat bad name

“Why don’t we run something on the text?”

Often conceptually flawed + noisy data + inadequate annotation schemes to create tools

Results can be cherry-picked by optimizing on the tool (so many available, broad range of quality)

But, used right it can be valuable research instrument

Sentiment Analysis Evidence

Individual text level (for example one tweet): not reliable it seems (MASON, OTHER DAVID PAPERS)

Individual person level: Promising, for example depression (EICHSTAEDT) or …

Group level: debated, for example heart disease thing, mixed results, method-depending in other cases (JAIDKA) paper

New: macroscopic validations

derstandard.at

An internet pioneer in the German speaking area (centered on Austria)

Highly active: almost 57 million visits in November 2020

Twitter

All Tweets from Austria (data on location from Brandwatch)

Mood Survey

Survey on yesterday’s emotional state for 20 days in November 2020

“How was your last day” (“Wie war der letzte Tag?”)

Was displayed in between the article text, low barrier, could be an answered anonymously

In a collaboration with derstandard.at, we obtained the survey results

Investigate the relationship of the explicit survey with the results of methods that extract sentiment from text

Text analysis

Combination of dictionary based and deep learning (RoBERTa) based sentiment analysis on the text of postings (in German)

LIWC and German Sentiment

These were the only two tools used, no cherry-picking of the methods (see preregistration)
